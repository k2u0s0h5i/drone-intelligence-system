DRONE CLASSIFICATION BY WEIGHT IN INDIA

Under the Drone Rules 2021, drones in India are classified into five categories based on their maximum take-off weight. Nano drones are the smallest category and weigh up to 250 grams. Micro drones weigh more than 250 grams and up to 2 kilograms. Small drones weigh more than 2 kilograms and up to 25 kilograms and are commonly used for commercial applications such as surveying and agriculture. Medium drones weigh more than 25 kilograms and up to 150 kilograms and are typically used for industrial, defense, and large-scale operations. Large drones weigh more than 150 kilograms and are mainly used for specialized and advanced applications. This classification determines registration requirements, pilot licensing, and operational permissions under DGCA regulations.
